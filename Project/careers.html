Maritime Careers

Explore different jobs in the maritime industry and learn about education paths. Discover what people do when they work at sea, in ports, or in marine-related fields.

Career Categories

Example of Maritime Jobs

Ship Captain

Leads the ship and crew, makes important navigation decisions, and ensures safety of vessel and cargo.

Education

Maritime academy training, Captain's license, years of sea experience

Salary Range

$80,000 - $200,000+ annually

Job Outlook

Steady demand with 5% growth expected

Marine Biologist

Studies ocean animals and plants, conducts research, and works on conservation projects.

Education

University degree in marine biology or related field, often Master's or PhD

Salary Range

$45,000 - $100,000 annually

Job Outlook

Growing field with 8% growth expected

Port Worker

Loads and unloads cargo from ships, operates port equipment, and handles logistics.

Education

High school diploma, on-the-job training, equipment certifications

Salary Range

$35,000 - $70,000 annually

Job Outlook

Stable with automation changing some roles

Ship Engineer

Maintains and repairs ship engines and equipment, ensures mechanical systems function properly.

Education

Technical training or engineering degree, marine engineering license

Salary Range

$60,000 - $120,000 annually

Job Outlook

Good demand with 6% growth expected

Coast Guard

Protects waters, enforces laws, conducts search and rescue, and ensures maritime safety.

Education

High school diploma minimum, special training programs, Coast Guard Academy

Salary Range

$40,000 - $90,000 annually

Job Outlook

Stable government employment

Marine Surveyor

Inspects ships for safety and quality, certifies vessels, and assesses damage or condition.

Education

Maritime education & experience, surveyor certifications

Salary Range

$50,000 - $110,000 annually

Job Outlook

Specialized field with consistent demand

Career Paths & Education

Educational Pathways

Maritime Academies

Specialized institutions offering degrees in maritime studies, navigation, and marine engineering. Typically 4-year programs.

University Degrees

Bachelor's and advanced degrees in marine biology, oceanography, maritime law, and related fields.

Vocational Training

Short-term programs for specific skills like port operations, diving, equipment operation, and safety certifications.

Getting Started

1 Self-Assessment

Identify your interests: Working at sea vs. shore-based? Technical vs. scientific?

2 Research

Learn about different maritime careers, requirements, and job prospects.

3 Education

Pursue appropriate education and training for your chosen career path.

4 Certifications

Obtain necessary licenses, certifications, and medical clearances.

5 Experience

Gain practical experience through internships, apprenticeships, or entry-level positions.
