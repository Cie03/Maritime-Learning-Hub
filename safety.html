Essential Safety Procedures

Fire Safety

All crew members must undergo regular fire safety training. Ships carry various types of fire extinguishers for different classes of fire.

  • Regular fire drills
  • Proper storage of flammable materials
  • Maintenance of fire detection systems

Man Overboard

Immediate action procedures when someone falls overboard. Time is critical in these situations.

  • Shout "Man Overboard" to alert others
  • Throw lifebuoy immediately
  • Keep visual contact with person
  • Execute recovery maneuver

Abandon Ship

When remaining on board poses greater danger than evacuation. This is a last resort measure.

  • Don life jackets
  • Gather at muster stations
  • Follow captain's orders
  • Take survival equipment

Medical Emergencies

Procedures for handling medical situations at sea when professional medical help may be hours away.

  • First aid training for crew
  • Medical kits on board
  • Telemedical assistance
  • Evacuation procedures

Regular Safety Drills

Safety drills are conducted regularly on all commercial vessels to ensure crew readiness in emergencies. The International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS) requires:

Weekly Drills

Abandon ship and fire drills must be conducted weekly

Crew Participation

All crew members must participate in drills

Documentation

All drills must be recorded in the ship's log

Emergency Signals

General Alarm 7 short blasts + 1 long blast
Fire Alarm Continuous ringing of bell
Abandon Ship 6 short blasts + 1 long blast
Man Overboard 3 long blasts
Distress Call MAYDAY on radio
Life Jackets

Purpose: Personal flotation devices designed to keep a person's head above water.

Types: Inflatable, foam-filled, hybrid

Requirements: Must be readily accessible, properly sized, and in good condition

Inspection: Monthly visual inspection, annual servicing for inflatables

Lifeboats

Purpose: Fully enclosed boats for evacuation during emergencies.

Capacity: Must accommodate all persons on board

Features: Self-righting, fire-protected, motorized

Drills: Must be launched and maneuvered quarterly

Maritime Radio Equipment

GMDSS: Global Maritime Distress and Safety System

Equipment: VHF radio, MF/HF radio, satellite communication

Requirements: Continuous watch on distress frequencies

Testing: Daily testing of equipment functionality
